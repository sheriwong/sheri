Insights deliver timely and practical recommendations to incident commanders and resolution teams, aiding them in promptly addressing and resolving incidents. Powered by AI algorithms, these insights propose actions such as involving resolvers dealing with comparable incidents and notifying affected service owners who have not yet engaged in the incident.

Research Goals

This research initiative aimed to explore the integration of insights features into the existing incident management process for customers. Using a prototype, we intend to:

  • Understand expectations surrounding the insights card, linked incidents section, and the insights panel/area.
  • Investigate expected interactions with insights, including when and where to display them.
  • Determine the optimal emphasis on insights to enhance user understanding.
  • Examine the timing and context for prompting users to rate or grade insights in how useful they are.
  • Specify actionable steps for users based on the insights provided.
  • Identify additional insights that would be valuable in enhancing the user experience.

Working Hypothesis

During incidents, the commander wants actionable insights for faster resolution. They can dismiss, rate, or snooze insights, with dismissed/snoozed insights accessible in the insights area. If deemed not useful, the commander can rate an insight accordingly, providing feedback.

Methods

I gathered data from user testing. 30 to 60 minutes iterviews were conducted with 4 customers, selected based on incident management console adoption. Items required for user testing were:

  • Script
  • Low-fidelity mocks
  • Prototype
Tagging and documentation was done on Dovetail after.

I conducted a benchmarking analysis on other companies incorporating AI suggestions within their products, aiming to gain insights into their user flows and experiences.

Improvements and Final Product

During user testing, it was observed that the status was not clear when using only the icon. To address this, despite limited space, I paired the icon with a label for clarity. Furthermore, I improved the experience of linked incidents by visually greying out already linked incidents, accompanied by informative tooltips for enhanced clarity.

The original intention was for the insights panel to have the capability to move freely across the screen, but due to budget limitations, it was adapted into a popover design. To distinguish from competitors, the insights icon was transformed from a lightning bolt to a lightbulb. Furthermore, thumbs up and down icons were chosen over arrows to avoid association with Reddit, where downvoting signifies disagreement and upvoting indicates agreement or liking.

To make insights popups more valuable and less bothersome, they now appear only once to users. I added a dismiss button because users found the previous trash icon confusing. Popups are limited to one at a time, with a minimum interval of five minutes between each. Users can also choose how long to snooze them for: 5, 10, or 15 minutes, or until the issue is resolved.
